# USMLE Step 1 Guide for IMGs

*Everything you need to know about registering, preparing for, and passing Step 1 as an international medical graduate.*

---

## 1. What is USMLE Step 1?

USMLE Step 1 is the first in the series of licensing exams required to practice medicine in the United States. It assesses whether you understand and can apply fundamental concepts of the basic sciences — anatomy, biochemistry, physiology, microbiology, pharmacology, pathology, and behavioral science — to the practice of medicine.

Since **January 2022**, Step 1 is scored as **pass/fail**. This was a landmark change that shifted the scoring burden to Step 2 CK for residency competitiveness. However, Step 1 remains a critical gateway: you **must pass it** to move forward in the USMLE sequence, and it is required for ECFMG Certification.

For IMGs, passing Step 1 also demonstrates that you meet a minimum standard of medical knowledge comparable to US-trained students. Some programs still ask about Step 1 attempts or whether you passed on the first try, even though the numeric score is gone.

## 2. Eligibility and Registration for IMGs

### Who Can Take Step 1?

You are eligible if:
- You are officially enrolled in or a graduate of a medical school listed on the **World Directory of Medical Schools (WDOMS)** that meets ECFMG eligibility requirements
- You meet the eligibility period requirements (see below)

### How IMGs Register (Through FSMB)

Unlike US medical students who register through NBME, **IMGs register through FSMB** (Federation of State Medical Boards):

1. **Create an ECFMG account** via the MyIntealth portal (formerly OASIS, updated in 2026)
2. **Apply for Step 1** through FSMB at [fsmb.org](https://www.fsmb.org)
3. **Pay the exam fee** — $695 (2026–2027 application fee) + **$210 region fee** if testing outside the US/Canada
4. **Receive your scheduling permit** — this contains your eligibility period and scheduling number
5. **Schedule your exam** at a Prometric test center via [prometric.com](https://www.prometric.com)

### Eligibility Period

When you register, you will be assigned a **three-month eligibility period** during which you must take the exam. You can request a one-time extension (for a fee) if needed.

### Attempt Limits

- You may take Step 1 **no more than 3 times within a 12-month period**
- You may not retake Step 1 if you have already passed it

## 3. Exam Format and Content

### Current Format (Before May 14, 2026)
- **One-day exam**, 8-hour testing session
- **7 blocks** of 60 minutes each
- Up to **40 questions per block** (max 280 total)
- 45 minutes of break time + 15-minute optional tutorial

### New Format (On or After May 14, 2026)
- **One-day exam**, 8-hour testing session
- **14 blocks** of 30 minutes each
- Up to **20 questions per block**
- 55 minutes of break time + 5-minute optional tutorial

The shorter blocks with fewer questions are designed to allow more frequent breaks and reduce cognitive fatigue. The total exam time and content coverage remain the same.

### Content Outline

Step 1 is organized along two dimensions:

**System-Based Organization:**
- General principles (biochemistry, genetics, nutrition, aging, immunology)
- Organ systems: cardiovascular, respiratory, renal/urinary, gastrointestinal, reproductive, endocrine, nervous, musculoskeletal, skin/integument, hematopoietic/lymphoreticular

**Physician Tasks/Competencies:**
- Application of knowledge to clinical scenarios
- Mechanisms of disease
- Diagnosis
- Principles of therapy
- Communication and professionalism

The full content outline is available at [usmle.org/step-exams/step-1](https://www.usmle.org/step-exams/step-1).

## 4. Scoring: Pass/Fail

Step 1 is reported as **pass or fail only**. No numeric score or percentile is shared with you or residency programs.

- **Passing standard:** Reviewed periodically by an independent committee
- **Score report:** You will see only "Pass" or "Fail" on your report
- **Performance feedback:** If you fail, you receive a breakdown of performance by content area to guide remediation
- **What programs see:** Programs see your pass/fail status and number of attempts

### Why Pass/Fail Matters for IMGs

- **First-time pass is now critical.** Programs cannot differentiate applicants by Step 1 score anymore, so they look at whether you passed on the first attempt. Multiple attempts are a red flag.
- **Step 2 CK carries the scoring weight.** Your Step 2 CK numeric score is now the primary academic metric programs use to compare applicants.
- **The floor is the ceiling.** Passing is the only goal — there is no advantage to a "high pass" since there is no numeric differentiation.

## 5. Study Strategy and Timeline

### Recommended Study Period

Most successful candidates dedicate **6–12 weeks of dedicated study** for Step 1, building on 1–2 years of preclinical coursework.

For IMGs who may have been out of medical school for some time, plan for **3–6 months** of preparation, especially if you need to relearn basic science foundations.

### Study Phases

**Phase 1: Content Review (Weeks 1–4)**
- Use First Aid for USMLE Step 1 as your master outline
- Supplement with subject-specific resources for weak areas
- Begin a question bank (UWorld or AMBOSS) at 1 block/day

**Phase 2: Question Bank Intensive (Weeks 5–8)**
- Complete 2–3 blocks of UWorld per day (40 questions per block)
- Review every explanation — even for questions you got correct
- Annotate First Aid with high-yield facts from question explanations
- Track your percentage correct by subject

**Phase 3: Self-Assessment and Weak Area Targeting (Weeks 9–12)**
- Take NBME self-assessments weekly
- Review incorrect answers and identify patterns
- Revisit weak subjects using targeted resources
- Take UWorld self-assessment exams

**Phase 4: Final Review (Last 1–2 weeks)**
- Rapid review of First Aid annotations
- Review commonly tested topics (rapid review section at the end of First Aid)
- Take one final NBME self-assessment 1 week before exam day

### High-Yield Topics

Based on exam content and student experience, these areas appear frequently:
- **Pathology** — the largest component of Step 1
- **Pharmacology** — mechanism of action, side effects, drug interactions
- **Physiology** — cardiovascular, renal, respiratory, endocrine
- **Microbiology** — bacteria, viruses, parasites, and their associated diseases
- **Immunology** — hypersensitivity, immunodeficiency, transplantation
- **Biochemistry** — metabolic pathways, vitamin deficiencies, genetic disorders
- **Behavioral science** — ethics, communication, biostatistics, development

## 7. Test Day Tips

### Before the Exam
- Confirm your Prometric appointment date, time, and location 1 week before
- Visit the test center in advance if possible
- Prepare your ID documents — they must match the name on your scheduling permit exactly
- Get a full night's sleep — do not cram the night before
- Eat a proper breakfast

### During the Exam
- Arrive 30 minutes early
- You will store personal belongings in a locker
- The exam interface includes a calculator, lab values reference, and mark/review feature
- **Pace yourself** — aim for ~1.5 minutes per question
- Use the mark feature for questions you want to revisit
- **Take your breaks** — step outside, eat a snack, stretch
- You can increase your break time by finishing blocks early

### After the Exam
- Results are typically available within **3–4 weeks**
- You will receive an email notification when your score report is ready
- Log into your MyIntealth/FSMB account to view results
- If you pass — congratulations! Move on to Step 2 CK preparation
- If you fail — review your performance feedback, adjust your study plan, and register for a retake

## 8. Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

### ❌ Starting UWorld Too Late
Many students wait until they finish content review before touching questions. **Start early** — questions ARE your content review. Use explanations to learn, not just test.

### ❌ Memorizing Without Understanding
Step 1 questions are clinical vignettes that require application, not recall. Understanding mechanisms helps you reason through questions you have never seen before.

### ❌ Ignoring Weak Areas
It is natural to focus on subjects you enjoy. But your weakest subjects offer the most room for improvement. Track your QBank performance and target the bottom subjects.

### ❌ Overusing Too Many Resources
Using 5 textbooks, 3 question banks, and 4 video series leads to diminishing returns. Stick to one primary resource per category and master it.

### ❌ Not Taking Full-Length Practice Exams
NBME self-assessments simulate the real exam. Skipping them means you will not know your readiness. Take at least 4–6 before your exam date.

### ❌ Cramming the Last Week
The last week should be **rapid review**, not new learning. Your brain needs time to consolidate. Relaxing slightly in the final days improves performance.

### ❌ Not Registering Early Enough for IMGs
IMGs have additional administrative steps. Start the registration process **at least 2–3 months before** you plan to test to account for processing times.

## 10. FAQ

### Q: Is Step 1 still required for ECFMG Certification?
**Yes.** Passing Step 1 is a requirement for ECFMG Certification. You cannot become ECFMG-certified without it.

### Q: Does my Step 1 pass/fail status matter to residency programs?
Programs can see whether you passed and how many attempts it took. A **first-time pass** is ideal. Multiple attempts may raise concerns, even in the pass/fail era.

### Q: What happens if I fail Step 1?
You receive a performance profile showing weak areas. You can retake the exam, but you must wait at least **45 days** (as of the current policy) and register again with a new eligibility period. Use the feedback to target your weak areas.

### Q: Can I take Step 2 CK before Step 1?
No. You must pass Step 1 before you are eligible to take Step 2 CK. The USMLE sequence must be taken in order (Step 1 → Step 2 CK → Step 3).

### Q: How long should I study for Step 1 as an IMG?
If you are currently in medical school or recently graduated, 6–12 weeks of dedicated study is typical. If you have been out of school for several years, plan for 3–6 months to rebuild foundational knowledge.

### Q: Is UWorld enough to pass Step 1?
For most candidates, UWorld combined with First Aid is sufficient. However, if your foundational knowledge is weak, supplement with Pathoma for pathology, Boards & Beyond for physiology, and Sketchy for microbiology/pharmacology.

### Q: How much does Step 1 cost for IMGs?
The 2026–2027 application fee is **$695** plus a **$210 region fee** if you test outside the US and Canada. Additional fees may apply for rescheduling within 30 days of your appointment.
