# ECFMG Certification: The Ultimate Guide for International Medical Graduates (IMGs)

For any International Medical Graduate (IMG) dreaming of practicing medicine in the United States, obtaining **ECFMG Certification** is the most critical milestone of your journey. Administered by the Educational Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates (ECFMG), a division of Intealth, this certification is the official stamp of approval showing that your medical education and clinical skills meet the rigorous standards required to enter a U.S. graduate medical education (GME) program (residency or fellowship).

This comprehensive, step-by-step guide is designed to help you navigate the entire ECFMG Certification process—reflecting all the major system overhauls, portal transitions, fee structures, and clinical skills pathway updates implemented for the **2025-2026** and upcoming **2027 Match** cycles.

---

## 1. What is ECFMG Certification?

### Why It Matters
An ECFMG Certificate is not just a piece of paper; it is a legal and academic prerequisite. You cannot participate in the National Resident Matching Program (NRMP) Main Residency Match, secure a U.S. residency position, or obtain a license to practice medicine in the United States without it. 

The certificate serves three primary purposes:
*   **Residency Eligibility:** Program directors use it to verify that you are fully eligible to start an accredited residency program (ACGME-accredited).
*   **USMLE Step 3 Eligibility:** You must be ECFMG-certified to register and sit for the USMLE Step 3 exam (unless you are a U.S. or Canadian medical graduate).
*   **State Licensure:** Every U.S. state medical licensing board requires ECFMG Certification as part of their credentialing process for IMGs.

### Who Needs It?
You need ECFMG Certification if you graduated (or will graduate) from a medical school located **outside** the United States and Canada. This rule applies regardless of your citizenship:
*   **Non-U.S. IMGs:** Foreign nationals who went to medical school abroad.
*   **U.S. IMGs:** U.S. citizens who completed their medical education abroad (e.g., in the Caribbean, Europe, or Asia).

## 2. Eligibility Requirements

Before spending any money, you must confirm that you and your medical school meet the strict eligibility criteria set by ECFMG.

### The World Directory of Medical Schools (WDOMS)
Your medical school must be listed in the **World Directory of Medical Schools (WDOMS)** (available at [wdoms.org](https://www.wdoms.org/)). However, simply being listed is not enough. 

### The ECFMG "Sponsor Note"
To be eligible for certification, your medical school’s profile in WDOMS must include an active **ECFMG Sponsor Note**. 
*   **What it is:** A specific text block on the "Sponsor Notes" tab of your school's listing indicating that students and graduates of the school are eligible to apply for ECFMG Certification.
*   **Graduation Year Check:** The Sponsor Note lists specific graduation years. You must have graduated during a year that is explicitly covered by this note. If your graduation year is not included, you cannot apply.
*   **How to check:** Search for your school on the WDOMS website, click on your school's name, navigate to the "Sponsor Notes" tab, and look for "Educational Commission for Foreign Medical Graduates (ECFMG)".

## 3. Step-by-Step Process

The ECFMG Certification and USMLE exam registration landscape underwent massive changes. Most notably:
1.  **MyIntealth launch:** ECFMG transitioned all account management, certification applications, and credential uploads to the integrated **MyIntealth** platform.
2.  **USMLE Registration Shift to FSMB:** ECFMG stopped managing USMLE exam registrations. Effective January 12, 2026, all USMLE Step exam registrations, scheduling, and fee collections for IMGs are handled through the **Federation of State Medical Boards (FSMB)** portal.

Here is the exact step-by-step workflow to get certified:

### Step 1: Establish Your MyIntealth Account
*   **Action:** Go to the MyIntealth portal ([myintealth.app](https://www.myintealth.app/)) and create an applicant account. 
*   **Cost:** **$110** (Account Establishment Fee).
*   **Outcome:** You will receive an ECFMG Identification Number.

### Step 2: Submit the Application for ECFMG Certification & Verify Identity (Form 186)
*   **Action:** Log into MyIntealth and submit the **Application for ECFMG Certification**. During this process, you must generate **Form 186 (Certification of Identification Form)**.
*   **Identity Verification:** ECFMG requires online notarization of Form 186 to prove your identity. This is completed electronically via **NotaryCam**.
*   **Process:** Upload your passport and a passport-style photo. You will join a brief live video call with a NotaryCam notary who will verify your identity and electronically sign the document. Once completed, NotaryCam submits it directly to ECFMG.
*   **Cost:** **$580** (Application for ECFMG Certification fee, which includes the NotaryCam fee).
*   **Validity:** Once approved, Form 186 is valid indefinitely.

### Step 3: Primary-Source Verification of Credentials
*   **Action:** Upload your official medical diploma and transcripts to the **MyIntealth** portal (which utilizes the **EPIC** system infrastructure for primary-source verification). ECFMG will contact your medical school directly to verify that your medical degree is authentic.
*   **Cost:** **$220** ($110 for your final medical diploma and $110 for your final medical transcript).

### Step 4: Create an FSMB Portal Account & Register for USMLE Step 1 and Step 2 CK
*   **Action:** Once ECFMG confirms your basic eligibility, navigate to the FSMB USMLE Portal. Create a student/graduate account.
*   **Cost:** **$75** (One-time FSMB account creation fee).
*   **Exam Registration:** Register and pay for USMLE Step 1 and USMLE Step 2 CK through the FSMB portal. Select a three-month eligibility period.
*   **Cost:** **$695** per exam (plus international test delivery surcharges of approximately $210 for Step 1 and $235 for Step 2 CK if you sit for the exams outside of the U.S. or Canada).
*   **Preparation:** Your medical school must verify your status as a student or graduate before FSMB issues your scheduling permit. This is done electronically via the Online Applicant Status and Information System (OASIS) or EMSWP, or via paper form if your school does not participate in electronic verification.

### Step 5: Pass the USMLE Exams
*   **Step 1:** Pass USMLE Step 1 (scored as Pass/Fail).
*   **Step 2 CK:** Pass USMLE Step 2 Clinical Knowledge (Step 2 CK), which provides a three-digit numerical score. 
*   *Note: The old Step 2 CS (Clinical Skills) exam was permanently discontinued in 2021. You satisfy the clinical and communication skills requirements via the ECFMG Pathway programs.*

### Step 6: Complete the Communication and Clinical Skills Requirements
*   **Action:** Pass the **Occupational English Test (OET) Medicine** exam to satisfy the communication skills criteria.
*   **Score Requirements:** You must achieve a minimum score of **350** in Listening, Reading, and Speaking, and a minimum score of **300** in Writing. These must be achieved in a **single test administration**.
*   **Pathway Application:** Apply for one of the six ECFMG Pathways through the MyIntealth Pathways Application Portal.
*   **Cost:** **$945** (Pathway application fee).

### Step 7: Receive Your ECFMG Certificate
*   Once ECFMG receives and verifies your passing scores on Step 1, Step 2 CK, the OET, your approved Pathway application, and your verified final medical credentials, they will issue your official ECFMG Certificate.

## 4. Pathway Programs

Since the elimination of the USMLE Step 2 CS exam, ECFMG uses six specific "Pathways" to evaluate the clinical and communication skills of IMGs. You must apply and be approved for **one** of these pathways to be eligible for certification.

| Pathway | Target Candidate | Key Requirements |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pathway 1** | Already Licensed to Practice Medicine | For applicants who currently hold, or have recently held (on or after January 1, 2022), a license/registration to practice medicine without supervision in any country. Must submit a Letter of Good Standing from their licensing authority. |
| **Pathway 2** | Passed an OSCE | For applicants who have not held a license but have passed a standardized clinical skills exam (an Objective Structured Clinical Examination—OSCE) that is required for medical licensure in a specific country. |
| **Pathway 3** | Medical School Accredited by WFME | For applicants who graduated from a medical school accredited by an agency recognized by the World Federation for Medical Education (WFME). |
| **Pathway 4** | Medical School Accredited by Other Agencies | For graduates of medical schools accredited by an agency that has been approved by ECFMG (often regional or national bodies not yet fully recognized by WFME, but satisfying ECFMG's educational standards). |
| **Pathway 5** | Joint Program / Dual Degree | For applicants who are students or graduates of a medical school that has a formal, dual-degree agreement with a U.S. LCME-accredited medical school. |
| **Pathway 6** | Evaluation of Clinical Skills by Physicians | For applicants who do not meet the criteria for Pathways 1–5. Your clinical skills must be directly observed and evaluated by licensed, board-certified physicians in **six separate, real-in-person clinical encounters** using the ECFMG Mini-Clinical Evaluation Exercise (Mini-CEX). |

## 6. Timelines

Completing this process takes time. Proper planning is essential to ensure your certificate is issued before the NRMP Match deadlines.

```
[Month 1] Establish Account & Submit Form 186 (NotaryCam)
   │
[Month 2-3] Wait for ECFMG basic approval & Verify Credentials
   │
[Month 4-9] Prepare for and take USMLE Step 1 (via FSMB)
   │
[Month 10-15] Prepare for and take USMLE Step 2 CK (via FSMB)
   │
[Month 16-17] Register and pass OET Medicine Exam
   │
[Month 18] Submit Pathway Application (MyIntealth)
   │
[Month 19-20] ECFMG reviews and issues Certification
```

### Typical Component Timelines:
*   **Account Setup & Identity Verification (Form 186):** 1 to 2 weeks.
*   **Primary Source Verification of Credentials:** 4 to 12 weeks (highly dependent on how quickly your medical school responds to ECFMG's verification request).
*   **USMLE Step 1 Prep & Exam:** 3 to 6 months.
*   **USMLE Step 2 CK Prep & Exam:** 4 to 8 months.
*   **OET Registration & Results:** 4 to 6 weeks.
*   **Pathway Application Processing:** 2 to 4 weeks.

### Recommended Start Window:
We strongly recommend starting the ECFMG Certification process at least **18 to 24 months before your intended residency start date**. If you wish to enter the March residency Match, your USMLE Step 2 CK scores and Pathway verification must ideally be finalized and submitted to ERAS by **late September** (when ERAS applications open to program directors).

## 7. Common Mistakes and Pitfalls

Many IMGs face frustrating, expensive delays because of avoidable errors. Watch out for these common traps:

1.  **Name Mismatches across Documents:** 
    *   *The Issue:* Your name on your passport, MyIntealth account, medical diploma, and FSMB portal must match **exactly**. Even a missing middle name, a minor spelling variation, or hyphenation can cause ECFMG or FSMB to reject your application.
    *   *The Fix:* Use your legal passport name as the source of truth. If your university documents use a different spelling, you must submit a formal name change request or a supporting "one-and-the-same" affidavit.
2.  **Delayed School Responses for Verification:**
    *   *The Issue:* ECFMG will not issue your certificate until they receive direct confirmation from your medical school. Many international registrar offices do not check ECFMG's electronic portal (EMSWP) regularly, stalling applications for months.
    *   *The Fix:* Once you submit your credential request, contact your medical school's registrar or international office immediately. Inform them that ECFMG is sending an electronic or paper verification request and offer to assist with any administrative steps they require.
3.  **Attempting USMLE Registration in the Wrong Portal:**
    *   *The Issue:* Attempting to register for Step 1 or Step 2 CK inside MyIntealth.
    *   *The Fix:* Remember that since January 2026, MyIntealth is for ECFMG profile management, credential uploads, and Pathway applications, while **FSMB** is the exclusive portal for IMG USMLE exam registration.
4.  **OET Sub-Test Single Administration Rule:**
    *   *The Issue:* Some applicants fail a single OET sub-test (e.g., scoring 330 on Writing) and attempt to "make it up" by retaking only that sub-test. ECFMG does **not** accept combined results from multiple OET test dates.
    *   *The Fix:* If you fail to meet the minimum score in any of the four sub-tests, you must register for and take the **entire** OET Medicine exam again in a single sitting.
5.  **Letting Pathway Approvals Expire:**
    *   *The Issue:* ECFMG Pathway approvals are only valid for a specific Match cycle. If you are approved for a Pathway but do not match in that cycle, your approval may expire, requiring you to re-apply (and pay the pathway fee again) or complete "Pathways Revalidation" for the next cycle.
    *   *The Fix:* Time your Pathway application during the specific year you plan to apply to the Match.

## 9. FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

### Q1: Can I apply for ECFMG Certification before I graduate from medical school?
**Yes.** You can (and should) establish your MyIntealth account, verify your identity with Form 186, and take USMLE Step 1 and Step 2 CK while you are still a medical student. You will apply for certification as a student. However, ECFMG will not issue your final certificate until you have graduated, uploaded your final medical diploma, and had it successfully verified by your school.

### Q2: Is the OET the only English language exam ECFMG accepts?
**Yes.** ECFMG does not accept IELTS, TOEFL, or any other English proficiency exams. You must take the **OET Medicine** version specifically. Other OET versions (such as OET Nursing) are not accepted.

### Q3: How long does my final ECFMG Certificate remain valid?
Historically, ECFMG certificates were valid indefinitely. However, certificates issued based on Pathway programs (Pathways 1–6) are subject to expiration. Your certificate is valid through a specific date (usually linked to the end of the Match cycle for which you applied). Once you enter an ACGME-accredited residency program and complete your first year of clinical training, your ECFMG Certificate is marked as **permanently valid**.

### Q4: What happens if my medical school doesn't use the electronic EMSWP portal?
If your school is not part of the ECFMG Medical School Web Portal (EMSWP) network, ECFMG will send paper verification forms via international mail or courier to your school’s registrar. This process takes significantly longer (often 2–3 months). You should encourage your school's administration to join EMSWP, which is free for medical schools, to drastically speed up processing times.

### Q5: What is the minimum score required to pass Step 1 and Step 2 CK?
USMLE Step 1 is graded on a **Pass/Fail** basis (the passing threshold roughly correlates to a score of 196). USMLE Step 2 CK is a scored exam; the minimum passing score is **214**. However, since Step 1 is Pass/Fail, your Step 2 CK numerical score is highly scrutinized by residency program directors.

### Q6: Can I apply for a Pathway before passing Step 1 or Step 2 CK?
You can submit your Pathway application and complete the OET before passing your Step exams. However, ECFMG will not process or approve your Pathway application until they have recorded passing scores for both Step 1 and Step 2 CK. It is usually best to apply for your Pathway during the match season in which you are actively applying.
